: Swimmies

If you follow this guide closely and don’t skip parts, it’s near impossible for you to really break something for good. This guide will show you to install two very solid and reliable brick-protection so in the unlikely event that something goes wrong nothing is lost.

You're losing: You’re Wii’s warranty, but since the last normal Wiis were manufactured in 2013 and Nintendo has 12 months of warranty on their systems by default, you most likely don’t have any warranty anymore anyway.
Your Wii will not lose any functionality.

You're gaining: The ability to backup and load your games from a USB device or SD card, to play Project M and other mods, to use emulators, to use dozens of homebrew applications and so much more!

  1. How to check your Wii's System Menu version
    1. Start your Wii
    2. In the Wii System Menu (default), go to Wii Options (bottom left button with "Wii")
    3. Choose Wii Settings
    4. You're now in the Wii System Settings 1
    5. In the top right you can see your Wii System Menu version, if it says 4.3(E/U/J) you should skip the entire updating step.
    6. If you have a J or a U (meaning Japanese and USA) Wii you can still follow this guide but you might have to make some small adjustments to your USB Loader later
  2. How to setup an internet connection
    1. This is the only step in the guide that requires an internet connection on your Wii. If you don't need to update your Wii, you also don't need to set this up.
    2. This is starting from the Wii System Settings 1. To get to this menu, follow the first step
    3. Click the arrow pointing to the right one time to get to Wii System Settings 2
    4. Select Internet
    5. Select Connection Settings
    6. Your Wii can have three WiFi connections. The one the Wii's supposed to use has an orange selector. If you don't know if you already have a working connection setup you can select it and hit Connection test.If you have a working connection already you can skip to 3. Updating your Wii
    7. Select a connection that says "None" on the right next to it (Empty slot)
    8. Select Wireless Connection
    9. Select Search for an Access Point
    10. Select Okay
    11. Choose your Wireless Router from the list
    12. Enter your WiFi password and hit OK, then OK again
    13. Press OK again to save the settings
    14. Press OK once more to test your connection
    15. You will be asked if you want to update your Wii, if you select Yes here you can skip 3. How to update your Wii You can select No to update manually.
      The new connection will automatically be the default connection now.
  3. How to update your Wii
    1. This is starting from the Wii System Settings 1. To get to this menu, follow the first step
    2. Click the arrow pointing to the right two times to get to Wii System Settings 3
    3. Click Wii System Update
    4. Click Yes
    5. Click I Accept
    6. Let it update. Don't power off the console! Depending on your internet connection this might take a while, just be patient
    7. If successful it should say "The Wii system update is complete (...)"
    8. Hit Okay
    9. Next you'll be asked if you want to "use the Wii Shop Channel and WiiConnect24"
    10. As these services are not supported anymore, choose No
    11. "Are you sure?" - Select Yes
    12. Click on Wii Menu

2. Preparing your devices

  1. Finding your Wii's MAC Adress (Why?) Wii
    1. This is starting from the Wii System Settings 1. To get to this menu, follow the first step
    2. Click the arrow pointing to the right one time to get to Wii System Settings 2
    3. Select Internet
    4. Select "Console Information"
    5. Record your Wii’s MAC Adress (or read it from the TV screen for the next step)
  2. Preparing the SD Card Computer
    1. Backup any data you still need from your SD Card and format it as FAT32 with the biggest Allocation unit size you can choose
    2. Acquiring LetterBomb
      1. Go to the HackMii Website
        Alternative
      2. Make sure “Bundle the HackMii Installer for Me” is checked
      3. Select 4.3E as the System Menu version
      4. Enter your Wii's MAC Address
      5. Fill in the captcha
      6. Cut any of the two wires (doesn't matter which one)
      7. Open the downloaded file and extract the contents to the root of your SD Card
    3. Putting the All-In-One package on your SD Card
      1. Open the downloaded file
      2. Extract the contents of the "SD Card" subfolder to the root of your SD Card
    4. Remove your SD Card

3. Installing the Homebrew Channel Wii

  1. Insert your SD Card and USB device into your Wii
  2. The USB device goes into the outer port on the back
  3. Go to your Wii System Menu and go to messages
  4. You will find a letter with the LetterBomb logo either “yesterday”, today or another recent date
  5. Open the letter (some white text on a black screen will appear)
  6. Wait the 30 seconds scam warning until you can continue
  7. Press 1 to continue
  8. You are now in the HackMii installer
  9. Depending on your Wii it will show different things behind BootMii:
    1. If you see Can be installed you can install BootMii as boot2
    2. If you see Can only be installed as an IOS, you can only install BootMii as an IOS
